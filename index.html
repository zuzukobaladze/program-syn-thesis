Program synthesis, the automated generation of executable code from high-level specifications, has long stood as a central goal in computer science. This thesis presents a comprehensive comparative literature review of the five major paradigms that have shaped the field:

  • Logic-Based (Deductive) Synthesis ‚Äî Formal proofs and theorem proving
  • Inductive (Example-Based) Synthesis ‚Äî Learning from input-output examples
  • Sketch & Schema-Based Synthesis ‚Äî Human-guided program completion
  • LLM-Based Synthesis ‚Äî Large language model approaches
  • Neuro-Symbolic (Hybrid) Synthesis ‚Äî Combining neural and symbolic methods

The review outlines each paradigm's motivations, mechanisms, seminal systems, and fundamental trade-offs‚Äî ultimately showcasing the transition from formal methods to statistical models and the ongoing quest to balance correctness guarantees with accessibility.
